Elon Musk and Dogecoin

by Dylan Herring

Posted on April 26, 2021

Despite the turmoil and confusion of this last year, living our lives in the shadow of a global pandemic, people still found entertainment and inclusion in some very strange ways. In the back corners of Reddit, a platform that calls itself “The Front Page of the Internet”, a small community of people partook in a strange hobby. This small community revolves around Dogecoin, a cryptocurrency similar to Bitcoin or Ethereum.

At least, similar in that it is a cryptocurrency, but that is the only similarity. Dogecoin is a Memecoin. If that doesn’t mean anything to you, don’t fret, we will get into the meaning of Memecoins as well. Over the course of 2020, Dogecoin rose dramatically in popularity, thanks in part to one very unusual and very well known supporter. Elon Musk. The elongated muskrat himself, ceo of Tesla and SpaceX, and one of the world's richest people. Elon Musk is also an avid Twitter user, and his tweets about Dogecoin had a huge impact on the price of the Cryptocoin. So, what is Dogecoin, how does Elon Musk relate to it, and what does it all mean?

First, to answer the question, what is Dogecoin, Cryptocoin, Memecoin, or any of the other terms used above. To start, a cryptocurrency is something like bitcoin, a digital currency that relies on computer “programs” called blockchain that contain a record of all the transactions that coin has been through. It’s almost like blockchain and bitcoin is a virtual hard drive of data about itself.

A meme is harder to define. Wikipedia defines a meme as “an idea, behavior, or style that spreads by means of imitation from person to person within a culture and often carries symbolic meaning representing a particular phenomenon or theme.” I would personally describe a meme as simply visual humor with an accompanying caption that serves as a punchline, or the setup of a joke. The image of the “Doge” is a template that a massive number of memes have been built off of. Therefore, a Memecoin is a joke coin. Dogecoin is just another meme built off of the Doge template. Dogecoin differs from other cryptocurrency in one very important way. It is suffering from extreme, and unending inflation. There is no cap on the number of dogecoins that exist, meaning the price per coin is constantly decreasing.

Elon Musk leaves no question that he likes dogecoin, through tweets about it over the last year. Elon Musk's tweets have the ability to influence the price of dogecoin. That's because when he tweets about it, thousands of people see it, and then they look it up, generating interest in it. Then they buy some on a whim, because everyone else is, and Elon Musk said he liked it, which drives the price up. Since the coin is in a constant state of inflation, the price can’t stay up for long, and as interest subsides, and the smart people sell, the price plummets again. As you can see in these graphs, there is a dramatic increase in the price of dogecoin, which experts have shown correlates to tweets by Elon Musk.

In conclusion, Elon Musk's tweets, and the interest those tweets generate, is really bad for the average person, because those tweets cause a lot of people to buy in on dogecoin, where they will almost definitely lose their money. However, this can be seen as a wakeup call. Don’t let yourself be drawn in by the next “Memecoin” or Meme-anything. It’s not very hard to look at a new fad or trend, and evaluate if it's worth your time. Most of the time, it won't be.
