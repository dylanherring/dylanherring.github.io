What got me started?

In my Intro to Business Real Estate class two years ago, I did a project on real estate mortgage qualification. It was this project that got me interested in the Real Estate profession. Through this project, I discovered that the process of researching a property drew upon the same skills as Information Science.

As I researched the Appraisal Profession, I learned about the USPAP, the three Real Estate Valuation Methods, and how an appraiser "assemble a series of facts, statistics, and other information regarding specific properties, analyze this data, and develop opinions of value."

-appraisalinstitue.org


The connection:

As an Information Science student, I learned to collect and assemble various types of data and developed the technical and analytical skills to derive meaningful conclusions and insights.

I have taken classes in information visualization and become skilled in communicating my findings in any format desired. All of the skills I have spent my college career developing and honing are skills used on a daily basis by Appraisal Professionals.
