Conditional Probability of Citations and Arrests

The data in the linked file above and in the visualization show the conditional probability of getting a citation, given that you are either getting arrested or not getting arrested, based on probabilities from the data frame. We can see that the probability of getting arrested and a citation are very low. This could be in part because an arrestable offense means there is no need for a citation. On the other hand, if you are not arrested, but you are stopped by police in any fashion, there is a 99% probability of receiving some form of citation. This makes sense, because if you assume that the majority of police stops are done because of someone breaking the law, in situations where an arrest is not performed, the lesser punishment for breaking the law is going to be a citation. Whats interesting is that from the entire dataframe, the total number of (Arrest=True & Citation=True) events was a total of 5 out of 6365 stops. That is a really small number, which is reflected in the visualization. Another question i would like to ask in the future is, what is the relationship between arrests, citations, and the type of stop performed. Are vehicle stops more likely to end in an arrest, a citation, both, or neither? It's worth investigating further.
